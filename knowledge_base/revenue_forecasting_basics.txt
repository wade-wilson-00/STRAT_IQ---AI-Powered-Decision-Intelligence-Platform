Revenue Forecasting Basics for SaaS

Revenue forecasting estimates future Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) and cash performance using historical and current operating signals.

Core concepts:
- MRR: normalized monthly recurring subscription revenue.
- ARR: annualized recurring revenue (MRR x 12).
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR): ability to retain and expand existing customer revenue.
- Gross churn vs net churn: revenue lost before and after expansion revenue.

Common SaaS forecasting inputs:
- Current MRR
- Historical growth rate
- Churn rate
- Expansion or upsell rate
- Marketing spend trends
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)

Simple directional forecasting:
Projected MRR is often estimated by combining growth and churn effects over a monthly horizon. Even simple models are useful when assumptions are clearly documented and reviewed regularly.

Forecasting best practices:
- Re-forecast on a fixed cadence (weekly or monthly).
- Use scenario bands: conservative, base, and aggressive.
- Separate topline projection from confidence level.
- Record assumptions and compare forecast vs actuals.

Common pitfalls:
- Treating one-time revenue as recurring.
- Ignoring contraction within existing accounts.
- Overfitting to short-term seasonal spikes.
- Failing to adjust assumptions after market changes.
