Revenue Forecasting Feature Reference

The following features are commonly used in SaaS revenue prediction pipelines.

1) current_mrr
Definition: current monthly recurring revenue baseline.
Why it matters: primary anchor value for forward projection.

2) mrr_growth_rate
Definition: expected monthly growth percentage from new and existing revenue.
Why it matters: drives trajectory slope in forecast windows.

3) churn_rate
Definition: monthly revenue loss percentage from cancellations or downgrades.
Why it matters: directly offsets growth and influences net retention.

4) expansion_rate
Definition: revenue expansion from upsells, cross-sells, and seat growth.
Why it matters: can compensate for gross churn and improve NRR.

5) months
Definition: forecast horizon in months.
Why it matters: longer horizons increase uncertainty and scenario spread.

6) previous_revenues
Definition: historical monthly revenue sequence.
Why it matters: captures trend momentum and volatility patterns.

Derived metrics often shown to users:
- net_revenue_retention
- annual_run_rate
- ltv_cac_ratio
- months_to_target

Feature quality checklist:
- Use consistent units and time windows.
- Clamp impossible values (for example negative MRR).
- Handle missing history explicitly.
- Validate percentage fields before model inference.
