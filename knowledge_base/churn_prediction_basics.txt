Churn Prediction Basics for SaaS

Churn prediction estimates the likelihood that a customer account will cancel, downgrade, or become inactive.

Why churn prediction matters:
- Retention is usually cheaper than acquisition.
- Churn strongly impacts LTV, cash flow, and growth efficiency.
- Early risk detection enables targeted interventions.

Typical churn outputs:
- Binary classification (churn vs non-churn)
- Churn probability score
- Risk tier labels (low, medium, high)

Common input signals:
- Product engagement and usage frequency
- NPS or satisfaction proxies
- Support ticket volume
- Contract term length and renewal timing
- Payment failures or billing issues

Operational best practices:
- Review model calibration, not just accuracy.
- Define action thresholds (for example >= 65% = high-risk playbook).
- Pair predictions with intervention workflows.
- Track post-intervention outcomes to close the loop.

Pitfalls to avoid:
- Using leaked features (data unavailable at prediction time).
- Ignoring class imbalance in churn labels.
- Assuming one threshold fits all customer segments.
- No monitoring for drift in customer behavior.
