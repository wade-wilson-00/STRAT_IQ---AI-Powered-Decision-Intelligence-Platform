Churn Prediction Feature Reference

This document explains common churn model features and why they are useful.

1) engagement_score
Definition: normalized account engagement signal from product activity.
Use: lower values usually correlate with higher churn risk.

2) nps_score
Definition: customer willingness to recommend product (0-10 scale).
Use: low NPS often appears in at-risk cohorts.

3) product_adoption_rate
Definition: proportion of key features adopted by account users.
Use: shallow adoption can signal weak product fit.

4) payment_failures
Definition: billing failure count within a recent period.
Use: recurring payment issues increase involuntary churn risk.

5) support_tickets_last_30d
Definition: recent support workload for an account.
Use: spikes may indicate friction or unresolved blockers.

6) customer_age_months
Definition: account tenure.
Use: early-life accounts can have different churn patterns than mature accounts.

7) cac_payback_months
Definition: months needed to recover acquisition cost.
Use: helps prioritize retention effort by economics.

8) active_users_ratio
Definition: active users divided by total licensed users.
Use: low utilization can precede contraction or cancellation.

9) contract_length_months
Definition: subscription term length.
Use: shorter terms can correlate with higher renewal uncertainty.

10) churn_risk_flag
Definition: existing rules-based risk marker.
Use: combines deterministic heuristics with ML probability for triage.
