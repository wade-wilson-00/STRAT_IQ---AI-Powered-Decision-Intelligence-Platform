DOCUMENT TYPE: Technical Reference — Churn Prediction Feature Definitions
PURPOSE: Defines each feature used in SaaS churn prediction models. For each feature, this document provides its name, a precise definition, the data it represents, and its predictive relevance. Use this document to answer questions about what specific model inputs mean and why they are included.

---

FEATURE DEFINITIONS

---

FEATURE: engagement_score
Type: Continuous (normalized, typically 0–1 or 0–100)
Definition: A composite, normalized signal derived from product activity events such as login frequency, feature interactions, and session depth over a defined lookback window.
Predictive relevance: Lower engagement scores strongly correlate with higher churn risk. An account that has recently become less active than its historical baseline is a leading indicator of disengagement and potential cancellation.
Usage note: Normalization method and lookback window should be consistent across training and inference.

---

FEATURE: nps_score
Type: Ordinal integer (0–10 scale)
Definition: The customer's Net Promoter Score, representing their stated willingness to recommend the product to others. Scores 0–6 are detractors, 7–8 are passives, 9–10 are promoters.
Predictive relevance: Low NPS scores (detractors) appear disproportionately in at-risk cohorts. NPS alone is not sufficient to predict churn but adds meaningful signal when combined with behavioral features.
Usage note: NPS data may be sparse if not regularly collected. Impute or flag missing values explicitly.

---

FEATURE: product_adoption_rate
Type: Continuous (proportion, 0.0–1.0)
Definition: The fraction of key product features that have been actively used by at least one account user within a defined recent period.
Predictive relevance: Shallow adoption (low proportion of features used) signals weak product-market fit at the account level. Accounts relying on only one or two core features are more vulnerable to switching if a competitor addresses that narrow use case.
Usage note: "Key features" must be defined and updated as the product evolves.

---

FEATURE: payment_failures
Type: Count (non-negative integer)
Definition: The number of billing failures recorded for the account within a recent rolling period (e.g., the last 60 days).
Predictive relevance: Repeated payment failures are a direct precursor to involuntary churn — where the customer is lost due to billing issues rather than dissatisfaction. Even one failure can signal account instability.
Usage note: Distinguish between payment failures that were subsequently resolved versus those that remain open when computing this feature.

---

FEATURE: support_tickets_last_30d
Type: Count (non-negative integer)
Definition: The total number of support tickets submitted by the account in the last 30 days.
Predictive relevance: A recent spike in support volume may indicate unresolved technical friction, poor onboarding, or growing user frustration. However, this feature must be interpreted carefully — very low ticket counts can also indicate disengagement rather than satisfaction.
Usage note: Consider pairing this feature with ticket resolution rate or sentiment to improve signal quality.

---

FEATURE: customer_age_months
Type: Continuous (non-negative integer or float)
Definition: The number of months since the account first became a paying customer.
Predictive relevance: Churn patterns differ significantly by account tenure. Early-life accounts (0–6 months) are at highest risk due to incomplete onboarding. Mature accounts (24+ months) often have lower churn rates but higher revenue impact when they do churn. Segment-specific thresholds may be needed.
Usage note: Do not treat this as a linear feature without testing non-linear transformations or segmentation.

---

FEATURE: cac_payback_months
Type: Continuous (positive float)
Definition: The estimated number of months required to recover the customer acquisition cost (CAC) from that account's recurring revenue.
Predictive relevance: This feature contextualizes the economic priority of retaining a given account. Accounts with long payback periods that also show high churn risk represent the greatest financial exposure. It is useful for prioritizing CSM intervention effort, not as a direct churn signal.
Usage note: Requires reliable CAC attribution data by account or segment. May not be available at the account level for all businesses.

---

FEATURE: active_users_ratio
Type: Continuous (proportion, 0.0–1.0)
Definition: The proportion of licensed user seats that have been active within a defined recent period (e.g., last 30 days).
Predictive relevance: A low ratio means the customer is paying for seats that are going unused. This underutilization is a strong leading indicator of contraction (downgrades) or full cancellation, particularly at renewal. It also signals that the account sponsor may struggle to justify the contract internally.
Usage note: Define "active" precisely (e.g., at least one login in the last 30 days).

---

FEATURE: contract_length_months
Type: Discrete (positive integer)
Definition: The length of the customer's current subscription contract in months (e.g., 1 = month-to-month, 12 = annual, 24 = two-year).
Predictive relevance: Shorter contract terms are associated with higher renewal uncertainty. Month-to-month customers can churn at any time with little friction. Annual customers are locked in but may not renew if value delivery is insufficient.
Usage note: Combine with contract end date proximity features for time-sensitive risk scoring.

---

FEATURE: churn_risk_flag
Type: Binary (0 or 1) or categorical (Low / Medium / High)
Definition: An existing rules-based risk marker generated by deterministic business logic (e.g., if active_users_ratio < 0.3 AND support_tickets_last_30d > 5, flag as high risk).
Predictive relevance: Acts as a complementary signal alongside ML probability scores. Rules-based flags encode business-specific thresholds that may not emerge naturally from training data. Combining this flag with an ML score improves triage accuracy and provides an interpretable fallback when the model is uncertain.
Usage note: Document the rules underlying this flag and update them when business logic changes to avoid silent staleness.
